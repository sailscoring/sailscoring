# Code of Conduct

Sail Scoring serves a community of volunteers — scorers, sailors, and club
administrators as well as developers. The project's spaces (issues, pull
requests, code review, project email) should be as welcoming as a good club.

**What's expected:**

- **Be respectful.** Disagreements happen — over rules interpretation more than
  most things — and are argued on the merits, never against the person.
- **Be patient and welcoming.** People arrive here with very different
  backgrounds: deep scoring knowledge and no git, or the reverse. Both are
  wanted, and nobody is belittled for what they don't yet know.
- **Assume good faith.** A blunt bug report or a wrong answer is almost never
  malice. Ask before escalating.

**What's not acceptable:** harassment or discrimination of any kind, personal
attacks, sexualised or violent language or imagery, publishing someone's
private information, or sustained disruption of discussion.

**Reporting:** if you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, email
**mark@hyc.ie**. Reports are handled confidentially. The maintainer may edit or
remove content that breaches this code and, for repeated or serious breaches,
block the person responsible.

This code is intentionally short. It borrows its spirit from the
[Python](https://policies.python.org/python.org/code-of-conduct/) and
[Django](https://www.djangoproject.com/conduct/) codes of conduct; if a
situation arises that this page doesn't cover, those are the sensibility we'll
apply.
