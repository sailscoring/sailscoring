For a software developer, **Sailwave** might look like a simple CRUD application for recording race times, but its internal logic is governed by complex domain-specific rules (the **Racing Rules of Sailing** or **RRS**).

Here is a glossary of terms found on sailwave.com, translated into concepts familiar to a software developer.

### 1. Data Structure & Hierarchy

* **Series:** The top-level container or **Project**. It contains all the races, competitors, and scoring configurations for a specific event.
* **Fleet:** A **Collection** or **Data Subset**. Competitors are grouped into fleets (e.g., "Lasers" or "Handicap Fleet") to be scored against each other.
* **Flight:** A **Shard** or **Batch**. In large regattas where there are too many boats for one start line, the fleet is split into "flights" (e.g., Yellow flight, Blue flight). They are later merged or used to determine "Gold/Silver" qualifying tiers.
* **Competitor:** The **Object** or **Row**. Key properties include `Sail Number`, `Helm Name`, and `Rating`.

### 2. Scoring & Logic (The "Business Rules")

* **Scoring System:** The **Algorithm**. You can choose "Low Point" (where 1st place = 1.0 point, like a "low-is-better" golf score) or "High Point" (where more points are better).
* **Appendix A:** The **Standard Library**. This is the section of the RRS rulebook that defines the default scoring logic. Most Sailwave setups are "Appendix A Compliant."
* **Discard Profile:** **Outlier Filtering**. Rules that automatically ignore a competitor's worst results after a certain number of races are completed (e.g., "1 discard after 4 races").
* **Redress (RDG):** A **Manual Override** or **Patch**. If a boat is unfairly hindered (e.g., by a race committee error), a "Jury" may grant redress, manually adjusting their score to an average of their other races.
* **Tie-break:** **Conflict Resolution**. Rules for determining rank when two competitors have the same total points (e.g., who had more 1st place finishes).

### 3. Normalization (Handicaps & Ratings)

* **Elapsed Time:** **Raw Data**. The actual duration the boat took to finish the race (Start Time - Finish Time).
* **Rating / Handicap:** A **Normalization Coefficient**. Since different boats have different speeds, they are assigned a number (e.g., 0.985).
* **Corrected Time:** **Processed Data**. The final value used for ranking: `Elapsed Time × Rating = Corrected Time`.
* **PY (Portsmouth Yardstick) / PHRF / IRC:** Various **Schema** or **Rating Engines**. Each uses a different formula to calculate the Rating coefficient.

### 4. Finish Codes (The "Exit Codes")

In Sailwave, if a boat doesn't finish normally, they are assigned a code. Think of these as **HTTP Status Codes** or **Exception Types**:

* **DNC (Did Not Come):** `404 Not Found`. The boat was registered but didn't even show up at the starting area.
* **DNS (Did Not Start):** `503 Service Unavailable`. The boat was there but didn't cross the start line.
* **OCS (On Course Side):** `403 Forbidden`. The boat crossed the start line too early (a "False Start").
* **DNF (Did Not Finish):** `Timeout / SIGTERM`. The boat started but didn't reach the finish line.
* **DSQ (Disqualified):** `Runtime Error`. The boat finished but was later removed from results due to a rule breach.
* **RET (Retired):** `Graceful Shutdown`. The boat chose to quit the race voluntarily.

### 5. Input & Integration

* **Sail Number Wizard:** An **Input Validator / Autocomplete** tool. Since recording finishes is high-pressure and fast-paced, this UI tool lets users type just the last few digits of a sail number to quickly match and record a finish time.
* **Publishing:** **Deployment**. Sailwave doesn't just "save" files; it "publishes" results by generating HTML files and optionally uploading them via **FTP/SFTP** to a website.
* **Rating File:** A **Configuration File** (.csv or .txt) containing thousands of boat types and their standard handicap values. It acts as a look-up table for the software.
